Joe Hand

I help create knowledge from information & data. More specifically, I am bringing community data collection tools to informal settlements worldwide at the Santa Fe Institute, in collaboration with Slum/Shack Dwellers International and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. We are also researching the role of slums and informal settlements in cities and their development, leveraging data from over 6,000 settlements.

Previously, I spent seven and a half years biking and getting wet in Portland, OR. I moved to Portland to attend Reed College where I majored in Economics and Math. I wrote my thesis on an economic model fueled by creative destruction, a topic that still interests me. More recently, I managed data visualization projects at Periscopic with clients including the Unicef, Yahoo!, GE, the Economic Policy Institute, and the Hewlett Foundation.

I spend my time reading, cooking, writing Python, lifting kettlebells, and walking outside.
